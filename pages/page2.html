In vitro study conducted to characterize cohesivity or
resistance to deformation of gels in FDA-approved shaped
gel breast implants. Six groups of cohesive gels (N = 20 for Allergan products, N = 7 for Mentor) were measured for material properties using the BTC-2000™, which applies a controlled vacuum, or negative pressure, to the test sample while measuring the resistance to deformation using a synchronized target laser. Each sample underwent the same test procedure; a window (1.0 cm diameter) was removed from the apex of the shell and the respective gel was tested.

A study to compare outcomes with Natrelle® 410 and Mentor® MemoryShape™. Outcomes data included physical breast measurements, quality-of-life metrics, and patient/surgeon satisfaction assessment. Instances of visible/palpable wrinkling/rippling were discovered by the patient, surgeon, or clinical nursing staff during follow-up visits. Follow-up ranged between 20 to 77 months (Natrelle® 410) and 16 to 77 months (Mentor® MemoryShape™). All subjects were treated uniformly.
